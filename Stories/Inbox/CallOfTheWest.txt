Call of the west

1800s american expansion into the west. 
Man in the city in some clerical job decides to leave. 
Buys a gun, equipment, describe in detail. Is leaving his children and wife behind. 
Stops into a church but doesn't pack the same punch as it used to. 
Notes that the cathedral itself was an attempt to communicate grandness, yet feels constrained compared to the horizon (open door is behind him). 
Notes that religion is a human concept and that this call is of nature itself, and thus of a higher power. 

Walks off into the desert.
